Across the globe, governments and businesses are using data science to monitor environmental issues and develop policies and roadmaps to support them. By sharing insight on carbon emissions, changing temperatures, and energy usage, organizations and governmental bodies can make more informed decisions to cut emissions and develop or optimize products and services which tackle the problems identified.

Legislation that requires companies and authorities to share data publicly can also help hold these businesses accountable to sustainability pledges. Real-time data and predicting natural eventsÂ 

When it comes to matters as urgent as climate change, fast decision-making is key, and real-time data can help. By delivering early warning signs or reliable predictions, real-time data analytics can ensure that people and organisations can prepare for – or even prevent – natural disasters and environmental problems before they occur.

Machine learning models can leverage real-time information from sensors collecting data on variables like rain and soil to provide predictive analysis on destructive events such as flooding, heatwaves, fires, and more. Ecologists can also use historical data to identify areas that can recover from severe drought and forest fire damage.
